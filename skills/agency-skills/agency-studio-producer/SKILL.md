# Studio Producer

Use this skill when the problem is portfolio direction, not day-to-day delivery execution.

## Best for

- Portfolio and initiative prioritization across multiple projects
- Strategic resource allocation and tradeoff decisions
- Framing executive updates around investment, risk, and outcomes
- Deciding which bets to accelerate, pause, or stop

## Workflow

1. Start from business goals, constraints, and available capacity.
2. Inventory active and proposed initiatives with:
   - expected impact
   - strategic importance
   - cost or resource burden
   - delivery risk
   - dependencies
3. Group initiatives into:
   - must-fund
   - opportunistic
   - pause or cut
4. Make tradeoffs explicit across ROI, focus, and execution risk.
5. End with a recommendation for where leadership attention and resources should go next.

## Output contract

Produce:
- portfolio view of active bets
- strategic ranking or investment tiers
- resource tradeoffs
- major risks and constraints
- recommendation on what to accelerate, pause, or stop

## Critical rules

1. Do not confuse strategic priority with current loudest request.
2. Prefer fewer focused bets over overloaded portfolios.
3. Say what should stop, not only what should start.
4. Distinguish strategic guidance from delivery detail.
5. Make assumptions about budget, headcount, and timing explicit.

## Starter prompts

- Rank these initiatives by strategic value, delivery risk, and resource cost, then tell me what to fund or cut.
- Turn this set of projects into a portfolio view with recommended priorities and tradeoffs.
- Write an executive-level recommendation on where the team should focus next quarter.

## Autonomous decision rules

**Use this skill when:**
- the user is choosing across multiple projects or initiatives
- the task is about portfolio focus, resource allocation, or strategic prioritization

**Do NOT use when:**
- the task is day-to-day delivery planning better handled by `agency-project-shepherd`
- the task is sprint or backlog ordering better handled by `agency-sprint-prioritizer`
